## Website Purpose
### Website Concept (Hiruna - rana0302):
Our website’s concept is a dog adoption platform designed to connect potential pet owners with dogs in need of loving homes. The purpose of the website is to simplify the adoption process by providing users with an accessible, organised, and user-friendly platform where they can browse available dogs, view there profiles, and book appointments to begin the adoption process. Many adoption centres still rely on outdated systems and find people who are in need of dogs through word of mouth or limited in-person visits, which can make it difficult for users to explore pets efficiently and compare options before making a decision. Our website addresses this issue by combining all necessary adoption information into one platform, allowing users to search and filter dogs based on characteristics such as breed, age and even get availability by location and closest to them. Each dog profile includes images, behavioral descriptions, and relevant health care information to help users make the correct decision. Additionally, the platform includes appointment booking and account creation functionality to streamline communication between adopters and the admins. Overall, the website aims to reduce barriers in the adoption process and increase the likelihood of dogs finding suitable forever homes.


### Target Audience Profile (Muhammad Saaim - saai0013):
The main target audience, according to the PACT analysis, are mostly university students of our pet adoption website. They all are technology proficient and smart learners who wants to use a simple, user friendly interface with precise commands. Their overall engagement at our website includes searching for different sorts of pets, comapring multiple options, filling out adoption form, thus combining all these tasks contributes to longer overall adoption application process. The context of use is mostly casual, using a laptop/mobile phone, usually involving talks with housemates and family before coming to a final decision. Regarding technology, they prefer to have an eye catching, secure, and responsive website with easy guidance and accessibility. Since they are occasional users, their overall experience must be involving and productive to ensure their repeated visits.


### Scope Statement (Zac - path0171):
Our website will be designed for those who live in South Australia and will provide many features that aid in connecting potential adopters with available pets in their area. Volunteers at the adoption centre will have admin access, which allows them to manage pet listings, messages and adoption forms. Adopters will have access to a user-friendly interface that allows browsing a wide range of pets across South Australia that contain relevant information such as breed, age, size, location and more.

Users will be able to sign in securely through two-factor authentication, and once in, will have access to many key features, such as the ability to save favourite pets, track their adoption applications, and complete an adoption form to apply for a pet, as well as update information or receive notifications for their adoption process. Other features will include the user’s ability to make donations to support the centre and to contact the administrators through a messaging system found on the contact page, where they can book an appointment to visit a pet in person.

Administrators will also have access to a dedicated dashboard, where they can access all their admin abilities, serving as an efficient management platform within the website. Features will include the ability to add, edit and remove pet listings, review and respond to adoption applications, as well as communicate with users through the messages page. The dashboard’s home page will also include quick access to recent messages, applications and pet listings to streamline workflow.

These features for both the users and admins provide an accessible and efficient platform that turns adopting a pet into a relatively simple task, while supporting communication between adopters and administrators.
